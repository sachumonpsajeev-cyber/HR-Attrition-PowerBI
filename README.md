# HR Attrition PowerBI Dashboard

A Power BI dashboard that helps HR teams understand **why employees are leaving** and **who is most at risk** — built using the IBM HR Analytics dataset.

---

## What This Project Does

This dashboard looks at data from **1,470 employees** and answers 4 big questions:

- How many employees are leaving overall?
- Who is leaving — what age, department, or role?
- Why are they leaving — is it salary, overtime, or job satisfaction?
- Which employees are most at risk of leaving next?

---

## Dashboard Pages

### Page 1 — Big Picture
Shows the overall numbers at a glance:
- 16.1% of employees left the company
- 237 employees total left
- Employees who left earned on average $2,000 less per month than those who stayed

### Page 2 — Who Is Leaving?
Breaks down attrition by age, gender, and education:
- Employees aged 18–25 leave at the highest rate (34.8%)
- Sales and HR departments have the most attrition

### Page 3 — Why Are They Leaving?
Shows the biggest risk factors:
- Employees doing overtime leave 3x more often (30.5% vs 10.4%)
- Employees with poor work-life balance leave at 31.2%
- Sales Representatives have the highest attrition rate at 39.8%

### Page 4 — Who Might Leave Next?
A risk score system that flags employees who are most likely to leave, so HR can take action early.

## Tools Used

- Power BI Desktop — to build the dashboard
- DAX — to create custom calculations and risk scores
- GitHub — to store and share the project

---

## Key Findings

1. **Overtime is the biggest risk factor** — employees working overtime are 3x more likely to leave
2. **Young employees leave the most** — the 18–25 age group has a 34.8% attrition rate
3. **Low salary = higher attrition** — employees who left earned $4,787/month vs $6,833 for those who stayed
4. **Sales Representatives** have the highest attrition of any job role at 39.8%
